<h2>What Should I Do With This GRE Vocabulary List?</h2>
<p>The first thing to do with this list is to look it over to identify any words you dont know or are confused by. If you arent 100% confident that you can use a word correctly in a sentence, study it! Of course, you dont need to study words that you are already totally solid on. That would be a waste of time.</p>
<p>Once you know which words you<em> dont</em> know, you need to learn them! We provide quick and easy definitions for memorization purposes. And the best way to memorize a lot of vocab words is to make flashcards! Luckily, <a href="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/PrepScholar-GRE-Vocab-Flashcards.pdf" target="_blank" data-wpel-link="internal"><strong>weve made them for you</strong></a>. If youd rather study your flashcards online, I recommend <a href="https://quizlet.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow" data-wpel-link="external">Quizlet</a>. You can make a free account and use various tools to study your own flashcard sets.</p>
<p>However, while a level of rote familiarity with the words is important, <strong>knowing how the words are actually deployed in context will help you on the test.</strong> To help with this, try <strong>reading publications with lots of GRE vocabulary,</strong> like <a href="https://www.theatlantic.com/" target="_blank" data-wpel-link="external"><em>The</em> <em>Atlantic</em></a>, <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/" target="_blank" data-wpel-link="external">the <em>New York Times</em></a>, and <em><a href="http://www.economist.com/" target="_blank" data-wpel-link="external">The Economist</a></em>. This will help you see how lots of words are deployed in actual writing. You can also use a plug-in for your browser that highlights GRE vocabulary words, like <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/pervasive-gre/oniippcgggpbkcdjekknhmoeambkohmn" target="_blank" data-wpel-link="external">Pervasive GRE for Chrome</a>.</p>
<p>So, once you know which words from the list you still need to know, you have two necessary prongs of attack. On one hand, you need to memorize the words and definitions with flash cards. On the other hand, you need to make sure you know how the words are used in context by finding articles where the words are deployed. In the next section, <strong>well discuss the best way to study vocabulary flashcards.</strong></p>

<h2>How Do I Study All These Flashcards?</h2>
<p>Now that you have the goods, how do you get them to deliver for you? Once you print out the flashcards, we advise <strong>using the waterfall method to study. </strong></p>
<p>Lets say that you have 100 flashcards in a pile.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-3973" src="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab1-300x93.jpg" alt="body_vocab1" width="600" height="186" srcset="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab1-300x93.jpg 300w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab1-600x187.jpg 600w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab1.jpg 726w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 984px) 61vw, (max-width: 1362px) 45vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>Youll go through the cards one by one. For each card where you know the definition easily, youll put it in your Know It pile. If you dont know the definition, put it in your Struggled pile.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-3974" src="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab2.jpg" alt="body_vocab2" width="602" height="189" srcset="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab2.jpg 707w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab2-300x94.jpg 300w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab2-600x188.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 984px) 61vw, (max-width: 1362px) 45vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>When youve gone through all the words, pick up the Struggled pile. Next youll go through this pile. For words where you know the definition, place them in a second Know It pile next to <strong>but not combined with the first</strong> Know It pile. For words you dont know, make a new Struggled pile.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-3975" src="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab3.jpg" alt="body_vocab3" width="600" height="186" srcset="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab3.jpg 700w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab3-300x93.jpg 300w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab3-600x186.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 984px) 61vw, (max-width: 1362px) 45vw, 600px" /></p><div class='code-block code-block-5' style='margin: 8px 0;'>

<p>Repeat this process, placing new Know It piles in a row from left to right. This creates your waterfall. Repeat this until the Struggled pile only has 4-5 words.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-3976" src="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab4.jpg" alt="body_vocab4" width="602" height="186" srcset="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab4.jpg 706w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab4-300x93.jpg 300w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab4-600x185.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 984px) 61vw, (max-width: 1362px) 45vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>Now, youll move back up the waterfall. Start with the last Struggled pile and <strong>go through it repeatedly until you know all the words in the pile.</strong> Then, add the most recent (the rightmost) Know It pile.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-3977" src="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab5.jpg" alt="body_vocab5" width="601" height="238" srcset="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab5.jpg 717w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab5-300x119.jpg 300w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab5-600x238.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 984px) 61vw, (max-width: 1362px) 45vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>Go through those words. <strong>If you miss any, go through the entire pile again until you get all of them right.</strong></p>
<p><img class="alignnone wp-image-3978" src="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab6.jpg" alt="body_vocab6" width="601" height="251" srcset="https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab6.jpg 711w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab6-300x125.jpg 300w, https://www.prepscholar.com/gre/blog/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2016/12/body_vocab6-600x251.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 85vw, (max-width: 909px) 67vw, (max-width: 984px) 61vw, (max-width: 1362px) 45vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>Seems brutal, but it will guarantee that you learn those words! Continue this process until you get all the way back up the waterfall. This means you know all the words!</p>
<p>This is the best method to drill vocab flashcards because you study the words you dont know as well much more than if you just studied the entire stack over and over.
